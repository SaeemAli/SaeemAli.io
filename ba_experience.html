Data Science Experience with British Airways

This virtual experience gives an insight into life in British Airways as a data scientist and the types of tasks you would be undertaking. It tests your skills in both analytics and predictive modeling. Click here to view my code.

The first task is to create a web scraper to retrieve reviews of British Airways from a flight review site, SKYTRAX. The script I created gathers not only the user's review but additional information like the flight class and purpose of travel. The purpose of gathering this data is to analyse thousands of reviews and get an idea of how customers viewed their experience with British Airways. To do this, I used a method called sentiment analysis which is to take some text to decide how positive or negative the text is. This helps us to understand the overall feeling customers had during their flight without having to read every review. Using the information from the web scraper, I broke down the reviews by seat class to see if the class the passenger flew on had an impact on their review.

This graph shows that the passengers in first class generally had a better experience than those in other classes. This indicates that British Airways should put more enphasis on improve the other classes which are seemingly falling behind in quality.

The second task was to find out how likely a customer is to book a ticket based on various factors. We were given a dataset of 50,000 bookings that customers either made or ended up not making. The factors to consider included number of passengers, country of booking, class and whether the customer wanted additional baggage. This was done using predictive modelling. I used the first 40,000 bookings to train the model, this gives it an understanding of how each factor influcences the customer's decision. Then it predicted the decision of the last 10,000 bookings with a high degree of accuracy. The goal of this task was to find out whether this dataset was useful in predicted a customer's decision. My model proves that it is useful as it could anticipate the user's decision in most cases, therefore making it a good indication of future bookings.
